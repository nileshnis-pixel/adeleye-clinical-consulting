Services

Practical support, not platitudes

Most advice for exhausted healthcare workers stops at "set boundaries and do yoga." That doesn't change your roster, your pay, or your path. These services do the deeper work.

Career Direction Coaching

One-on-one sessions to get clear on where you actually want your healthcare career to go — clinical specialisation, education, management, or something outside the hospital entirely — and map the realistic steps to get there.

Skills & Advancement Planning

Identify the skills, postgraduate options and experience that move you toward the role you want — not just the ones your current employer needs filled. Build a plan you can act on around shift work.

Advocacy & Negotiation Support

Prepare for the conversations that change things: negotiating your roster, applying for promotion, making the case for flexibility. Learn to advocate for yourself the way you advocate for your patients.

The approach

Strategy first. Then the stress goes down.

Coaching follows a simple, structured path — because vague goals produce vague careers.

Get clear

What do you actually want — promotion, a specialty, flexibility, leadership? We name it precisely, because you can't plan for a destination you haven't chosen.

Build what matters

We identify the skills and experience that serve your goal, and sequence them realistically around the life and shifts you already have.

Control the narrative

You learn to advocate for yourself — in applications, interviews and roster conversations — instead of hoping someone notices your work.

Review and adjust

Careers aren't set-and-forget. We check what's working, adjust the plan, and keep momentum through the messy middle.

About

Coaching from inside the profession

Adeleye Clinical Consulting was founded by Nileshni (Nish) Adeleye, a nurse who works across two of the most demanding corners of Australian healthcare: a high dependency unit in a private hospital, and residential aged care.

That means the advice here isn't theoretical. It comes from someone who knows what a 12-hour shift takes out of you, what after-hours management actually involves, and how easy it is to spend five years in the same role because nobody ever showed you the other paths.

The belief behind the practice is simple: healthcare professionals don't burn out because they're weak — they burn out because they're unstrategic. Clarity, skills and self-advocacy fix what self-care alone never will.
